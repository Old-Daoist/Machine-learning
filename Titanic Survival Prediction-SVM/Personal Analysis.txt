The strongest predictors of survival in order of importance were: 
1.Gender (women survived far more than men) 

2.Ticket class (higher class meant dramatically better chances), and fare paid (even within the
same class, those who paid more were slightly better positioned).

3.Age mattered mainly for children —
young children had better survival odds. 

4.Family size showed a nuanced pattern: being
completely alone was a disadvantage in lower classes, but travelling with a moderately sized family (2–4
people) provided some benefit. Very large families fared poorly
